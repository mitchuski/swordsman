## Control Summary

### Swordsman (Right Side = Master = Right Click)

| Input | Action |
|-------|--------|
| Right-click **hold** | View/choose stance (the master deliberates) |
| Right-click **release** | Cast stance, leaves its mark |
| `S` key | Full stance editor |

The Swordsman holds a **posture**—a persistent stance that represents your sovereignty.

### Mage (Left Side = Emissary = Left Click)

| Input | Action |
|-------|--------|
| Left-click **tap** | Cast last selected spell (quick action, emissary executes) |
| Left-click **hold** | View all spells in orbit |
| `M` key | Full spell editor |

The Mage casts **spells**—discrete actions that project outward onto the page.

## Why This Works

1. **Natural Hand Position**: Most users rest their index finger on left-click (primary action) and middle finger near right-click (secondary/context).

2. **Frequency Matching**: Spells are cast frequently (left-click = primary), stances are set occasionally (right-click = deliberate).

3. **Mental Model**:
   - The **Mage** is your reactive agent—tap to act, the emissary executes your will immediately
   - The **Swordsman** is your deliberate guardian—hold to consider, release to commit to a stance

4. **Brain Hemisphere Alignment**:
   - Right hand → Left brain → Sequential/Action → Mage spells
   - But we flip it: Right-click is *right brain* (holistic) for the Swordsman
   - This creates interesting cross-wiring that feels intuitive once learned
